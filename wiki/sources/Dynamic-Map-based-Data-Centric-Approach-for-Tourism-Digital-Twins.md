# Dynamic Map-based Data-Centric Approach for Tourism and Cultural Heritage Preservation Digital Twins

## Abstract

This paper proposes a QGIS-based, data-centric approach to digital twin frameworks that supports management, development, and deployment of tourism and cultural heritage services in smart cities. The approach is validated through a case study on Salvador Historic Center - Pelourinho, a major cultural asset facing challenges in sustaining tourism flux while safeguarding heritage.

## Context

### Tourism Impact
**Positive:**
- Job creation
- Revenue generation
- Tax revenue
- Economic prosperity

**Negative:**
- Overtourism
- Pressure on housing and real estate markets

### Cultural Heritage Challenges
- Physical degradation
- Commodification
- Loss of authenticity

## Approach

### QGIS-Based Digital Twin
- Data visualization
- Data silos integration
- Heritage preservation support
- Overtourism control
- Urban resilience measures
- Climate adaptation

## Case Study: Pelourinho (Salvador, Brazil)

### Challenges Addressed
1. Sustaining tourism flux
2. Safeguarding cultural assets
3. Controlling overtourism
4. Implementing resilience measures

### Preliminary Results
- Effective data visualization
- Silos integration
- Adequate management support
- Heritage conservation enablement
