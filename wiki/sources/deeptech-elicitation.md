# Elicitation - Social Engineering Technique

## Summary

Elicitation is a social engineering technique that exploits human psychological factors to extract information through subtle, low-risk conversations. It leverages desires for politeness, professional recognition, validation, and concern for others to build rapport and encourage disclosure without coercion.

## Key Concepts

- **Core mechanism**: Exploits inherent human desires (politeness, recognition, curiosity)
- **Key factors**: Professional pride, praise psychology, social connection, low perceived risk
- **Applications**: Intelligence gathering, information extraction, relationship building
- **Characteristics**: Subtle, adaptable, stealthy, relationship-focused
- **Ethical note**: Powerful but ethically complex - depends on context and consent

## Connections

- Related to OSINT techniques for information gathering
- Complements personality analysis patterns
- Used in investigative and intelligence workflows
