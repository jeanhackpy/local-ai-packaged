# Online Advertising with Spatial Interactions

## Abstract

Online advertising platforms must decide how to allocate multiple ads across limited screen real estate, where each ad's effectiveness depends not only on its own placement but also on nearby ads competing for user attention. This paper introduces a framework for spatial externalities in online advertising where ad value depends on both slot and surrounding ad configuration.

## Key Contributions

1. **Nearest-Neighbor Model**: Polynomial-time algorithm achieving constant approximation for general cases. The allocation rule is monotone and implementable as a truthful mechanism.

2. **2D Euclidean Space**: PTAS (Polynomial Time Approximation Scheme) provided for structured settings.

3. **Product-Distance Model**: Strong hardness of approximation established - no polynomial-time algorithm can achieve polynomial-factor approximation unless P=NP (reduction from Max-Independent-Set).
