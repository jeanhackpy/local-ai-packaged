 Elicitation works effectively because it takes advantage of key human factors that influence our communication, behavior, and information sharing. These factors include:

1. Desire to be polite: Humans naturally want to be friendly and polite, especially to strangers or people in positions of authority. This desire can make them more willing to engage in a conversation and share information when approached with care and respect.
2. Professionalism: Professionals pride themselves on being well-informed and intelligent. When someone asks insightful questions, they may feel impressed and inclined to provide more detailed responses.
3. Praise and recognition: Humans have a psychological need for validation and positive feedback. When elicitation techniques are used skillfully, targets may feel praised and appreciated for their knowledge or expertise, leading them to reveal more information.
4. Concern for others: People respond positively to those who appear genuinely concerned about their well-being or interests. Elicitation can create an environment where the target feels listened to and understood, making them more willing to open up and share information.
5. Human nature's inherent curiosity: Humans have a natural curiosity that drives us to learn and explore new things. When elicitation is employed subtly, it can tap into this innate desire to gather knowledge or satisfy our curiosity, leading targets to reveal valuable information without even realizing it.
6. Social engineering: Elicitation is an essential aspect of social engineering – the art of manipulating human behavior to achieve a desired outcome. By understanding the motivations and desires that drive people, elicitation can be used to create a rapport with the target and build trust, making them more likely to divulge sensitive or confidential information.
7. Low risk: Elicitation is typically low risk as it does not involve coercion, threats, or force. This makes it an attractive option for gathering intelligence or uncovering valuable insights without resorting to aggressive methods that could potentially damage relationships or reputations.
8. Adaptability and flexibility: Elicitation techniques can be easily adapted and customized depending on the specific situation, making them versatile and effective in various contexts. From casual conversations at social events to more formal interviews, elicitation can be used to gather useful information from a wide range of sources.
9. Subtlety and stealth: Elicitation is often subtle and hard to detect, which makes it an effective tool for gathering sensitive or confidential information without the target's knowledge or suspicion. This stealthy approach allows elicitors to gather valuable insights while maintaining a professional and respectful demeanor.
10. Human connection: At its core, elicitation is about building a rapport with others and engaging in meaningful conversations that foster understanding, empathy, and trust. By using elicitation techniques skillfully, individuals can create connections that last beyond the initial interaction, leading to long-term benefits for both parties involved.
