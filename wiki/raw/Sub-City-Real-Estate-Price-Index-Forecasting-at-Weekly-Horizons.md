# Sub-City Real Estate Price Index Forecasting at Weekly Horizons Using Satellite Radar and News Sentiment

## Abstract

This study examines whether sub-city price indices can be forecasted at weekly frequency by combining physical development signals from satellite radar with market narratives from news text. Using over 350,000 transactions from Dubai Land Department (2015-2025), weekly price indices are constructed for 19 sub-city regions and evaluated for forecasts from 2 to 34 weeks ahead.

## Key Findings

### Horizon-Dependent Results
- **Up to 10 weeks**: Price history alone matches multimodal configurations
- **Beyond 14 weeks**: Sentiment and SAR (Synthetic Aperture Radar) become critical
- **Long horizons (26-34 weeks)**: Full multimodal model reduces mean absolute error from 4.48 to 2.93 (35% reduction)

### Data Sources
1. **Regional transaction history** (350,000+ transactions)
2. **Sentinel-1 SAR backscatter** (satellite radar)
3. **News sentiment** (lexical tone + semantic embeddings)
4. **Macroeconomic context**

### Technical Insights
- Nonparametric learners consistently outperform deep architectures in this data regime
- Gains are statistically significant across regions
