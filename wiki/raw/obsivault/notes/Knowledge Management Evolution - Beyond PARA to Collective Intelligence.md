At some point in the development of any idea, we will inevitably need the assistance of others. This necessity extends to our tools; they are not used in isolation but in concert with productivity and knowledge management systems. This is particularly true for knowledge workers, who must rely on a process because our brains cannot retain all the information we need daily. In its simplest form, this process involves writing notes and creating lists on paper with a pen or pencil. The paper is easy and cheap to share with others and isn’t read-only, allowing immediate feedback to be written directly. This feedback is often more effective for clarity when marked in a different colour.

A similar process occurs when we use a whiteboard collaboratively, but there’s an additional layer of complexity. We often visualise an image before transcribing it onto a whiteboard or paper. This ability is attributed to our [Visuospatial Working Memory](https://www.perplexity.ai/page/What-is-Visuospatial-FzgmKG3uRzOqZe8KP7nMmA) (VSWM), an extraordinary part of our brains that integrates reason and logic with memory, allowing us to interpret information visually and temporally.
