## **Psychology Glossary (A to Z)**

_Written and peer-reviewed by PhD-Level Academics_

___

### Accommodation and Assimilation

**Definition:** Accommodation and assimilation are terms from cognitive theory refer to the processes of adding and amending information in mental schema.

[![assimilation vs accommodation definitions examples](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/assimilation-vs-accommodation-definitions-examples-1024x724.jpg)](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/assimilation-vs-accommodation-definitions-examples-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Attachment Theory

**Definition:** Attachment theory explores the stages of relationships between caregivers and their children and proposes a range of attachment styles.

[![attachment theory examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/attachment-theory-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/attachment-theory-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Attribution Theory

**Definition:** Attribution theory believes that people attempt to understand events and actions by (often mistakenly) attributing intentions, beliefs, and feelings to the events.

[![attribution theory examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/attribution-theory-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/attribution-theory-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Classical Conditioning

**Definition:** Classical conditioning refers to learned behaviors that occur when a neutral stimulus is associated with a learned response.

![classical conditioning examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/classical-conditioning-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Cognitive Bias

**Definition:** A cognitive bias is a mental shortcut (also known as a heuristic) leading to an unconscious error in thinking.

![cognitive bias examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/cognitive-bias-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Concrete Operational Stage of Development

**Definition:** The concrete operational stage is the third stage in Piaget’s stages of cognitive development typically occurring between ages 7 and 12.

![concrete operational stage examples](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/concrete-operational-stage-examples-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Crystallized Intelligence

**Definition:** Crystallized intelligence refers to the knowledge and skills a person develops throughout their lifespan. It is opposed to fluid intelligence, which refers to on-the-spot problem-solving intelligence.

![crystallized intelligence vs fluid intelligence](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/crystallized-intelligence-vs-fluid-intelligence-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Fixed Ratio Schedules

**Definition:** A fixed ratio schedule of reinforcement is a rewards schedule when a reward is delivered after a specific number of responses.

![fixed ratio schedule examples strengths weaknesses](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/fixed-ratio-schedule-examples-strengths-weaknesses-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Fluid Intelligence

**Definition:** Fluid intelligence is the ability to solve newly encountered problems based on logic and reason.

![crystallized intelligence vs fluid intelligence](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/crystallized-intelligence-vs-fluid-intelligence-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Formal Operational Stage of Development

**Definition:** The formal operational stage is the fourth and final stage in Piaget’s theory of cognitive development that occurs from the ages of 11 and up.

![formal operational stage examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/formal-operational-stage-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Functional Fixedness

**Definition:** Functional fixedness refers to rigidity in problem-solving, characterized by a tendency to think of things only in terms of their usual functions.

![functional fixedness examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/functional-fixedness-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Higher Order Conditioning

**Definition:** Higher-order conditioning occurs when a second conditioned stimulus becomes associated with an initial conditioned stimulus and evokes an unconditioned response by itself. Confused? This article will clear it up.

![higher order conditioning definition and example](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/higher-order-conditioning-definition-and-example-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Intrinsic Motivation

**Definition:** Intrinsic motivation occurs when you do a task without any external incentives.

![intrinsic vs extrinsic motivation definitions and examples](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/intrinsic-vs-extrinsic-motivation-definitions-and-examples-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Kohlberg’s Theory of Moral Development

**Definition:** Kohlberg devised a theory of moral development which postulates that moral reasoning passes through six stages from early childhood to adulthood.

![kohlbergs theory of moral development](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/kohlbergs-theory-of-moral-development-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Locus of Control

**Definition:** An external locus of control refers to the belief that actions do not control outcomes. An internal locus of control refers to the belief that your actions can influence outcomes.

![locus of control theory definition and examples](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/locus-of-control-theory-definition-and-examples-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Metacognitive Theory

**Definition:** Metacognitive Theory is a theory of knowledge that is interested in how humans can actively monitor and regulate their own thought processes.

![metacognitive theory definition types](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/metacognitive-theory-definition-types-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Moral Relativism

**Definition:** Moral relativism is a view that rejects the existence of any objective, absolute or universal moral truths that govern our morality.

![moral relativism examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/moral-relativism-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Operant Conditioning

**Definition:** Operant conditioning explains how people and animals develop learned responses through the repetition of reinforcements and punishments.

![operant conditioning examples and definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/operant-conditioning-examples-and-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Positive Reinforcement

**Definition:** Positive reinforcement is a concept from conditioning theory that refers to the use of rewards and praise to encourage repetition of desired behaviors.

![positive reinforcement examples and definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/positive-reinforcement-examples-and-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Preoperational Stage of Development

**Definition:** The preoperational stage is the second stage in Jean Piaget’s theory of cognitive development. This stage is from ages 2 to 7 years old.

![preoperational stage examples definition substages](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/preoperational-stage-examples-definition-substages-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Rehearsal (Elaborative and Maintenance)

**Definition:** Elaborative rehearsal refers to strategies for committing information to long-term memory. Maintenance rehearsal is for short-term memory.

![elaborative and maintenance rehearsal descriptions](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/elaborative-and-maintenance-rehearsal-descriptions-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Reverse Psychology

**Definition:** Reverse psychology refers to a persuasion technique in which one person tries to get someone to do something by suggesting that they do the opposite.

![reverse psychology examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/reverse-psychology-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Schema

**Definition:** A cognitive schema is a mental framework that organizes information in the mind. It is a packet of mental information that categorizes objects and concepts.

![cognitive schema examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/cognitive-schema-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Selective Attention

**Definition:** Selective attention refers to the ability to intentionally focus awareness on specific aspects of a situation or information and drown out other information.

![selective attention example definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/selective-attention-example-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs

**Definition:** Self-actualization is the realization or fulfillment of one’s full talents and potentialities. It is the process of fully developing oneself physically, mentally, and spiritually.

![maslows hierarchy of needs](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/maslows-hierarchy-of-needs-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Self-Determination Theory

**Definition:** Self-determination theory (SDT) is a theory of motivation. ‘Self-determination’ is the sense that we are on control of our own lives and choices, are motivated, and can meet our potential.

![self determination theory definition examples](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/self-determination-theory-definition-examples-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Sensorimotor Stage of Development

**Definition:** According to Piaget, the sensorimotor stage of development is a child’s first stage of development. This stage lasts from birth to 2 years old.

![sensorimotor stage examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/sensorimotor-stage-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Social Penetration Theory

**Definition:** Social penetration theory explores stages of relationship progression and what happens in each stage.

![social penetration theory](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/social-penetration-theory-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Sublimation

**Definition:** Sublimation involves channeling unwanted or disturbing thoughts, impulses or desires into something positive and constructive.

![sublimation in psychology definition examples](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/sublimation-in-psychology-definition-examples-1024x724.jpg)

___

### Vicarious Punishment

**Definition:** Vicarious punishment is when one person observes another person (the model) being punished after doing something.

![vicarious punishment examples definition](https://helpfulprofessor.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/vicarious-punishment-examples-definition-1024x724.jpg)
